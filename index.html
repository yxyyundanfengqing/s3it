Abstract

Embodied agents are evolving from physical task executors to social collaborators in human environments. This evolution demands what we term embodied social intelligence: the ability to comprehend and navigate complex, implicit social norms while adhering to physical constraints. However, a critical gap pervades current research paradigms. To address this challenge, we introduce Spatially Situated Social Intelligence Test (SÂ³IT), a benchmark specifically designed to evaluate embodied social intelligence. It is centered on a novel and challenging seat-ordering task, requiring an agent to arrange seating in a 3D environment for a group of large language model-driven (LLM-driven) NPCs with diverse identities, preferences, and intricate interpersonal relationships. Leveraging the proposed benchmark, we evaluates state-of-the-art LLMs, uncovering significant deficiencies in their grasp of social norms, including social relations, abstract preferences, and conflict resolution, as well as spatial perception within embodied environments.

Testing Pipeline

Phase I: NPC Preference Extraction and Summarization

To emulate the real-world process of understanding others' needs, this phase constructs a structured preference profile for each NPC by extracting and summarizing information from simulated dialogues. The agent proactively elicits preferences through natural language interaction and distills the unstructured dialogue into structured profiles, including a description and an intensity score.

Phase II: Environmental Cognition

This phase evaluates the agent's efficacy in exploring the simulated environment and converting raw visual inputs into structured representations. The agent perceives and interacts with the environment through a target-driven, iterative exploration process, dynamically refining its understanding of environmental features like seat properties and spatial relationships.

Phase III: Multi-Constraint Decision-Making

This phase assesses the agentâ€™s higher-order social reasoning under complex physical and social constraints. It uses an iterative "generate-and-reflect" optimization loop, where the agent generates a seating plan, reflects on its quality, and refines the solution by leveraging both static context (profiles, environment) and dynamic feedback from previous iterations.
